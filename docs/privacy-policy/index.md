# Privacy Policy for Math Master

Effective date: 2 June 2026  
Last updated: 2 June 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how Scholtz & Company, jsa, ICO 51882272
("Developer", "we", "us", or "our") handles information in connection with
the Math Master application (the "App"). The App is an educational mathematics
game designed for children and families. It is intended to be safe for kids: it
does not contain advertising, behavioral tracking, social networking features,
public chat, in-app disclosure of personal information, or any feature intended
to cause a child to share personal data with us or with other users.

This Policy is written to explain our practices in a clear and complete manner
for users, parents, guardians, schools, and app-store review teams, including
Google Play Console review. The most important point is simple: the App stores a
player nickname and game activity locally on the device so the game can work,
but no personal data leaves the device through the App.

## 1. Developer and scope

The App is created and provided by Scholtz & Company, jsa, ICO 51882272. This
Policy applies only to the Math Master App and to information processed by the
App itself. It does not govern Google Play, the device operating system, app
store account management, device backups, parental-control tools, or other
third-party services that may be operated independently from the App.

The App is a local educational product. It helps children practise arithmetic,
records their progress on the device, and allows several local player profiles
to be used on the same device. We do not operate a cloud account system for the
App, and the App does not require a child to create an online account.

## 2. Clear child-safety statement

The App is designed to be appropriate for children. It may be used by children
with the involvement of a parent, guardian, teacher, or other responsible adult.
The App does not ask for a child's full name, email address, phone number,
postal address, photograph, voice recording, precise location, school name,
contact list, payment information, advertising identifier, or any other
unnecessary personal information.

The player name entered in the App can be any nickname, initials, or invented
name chosen by the user. We recommend that children do not enter their full legal
name. The nickname is used only inside the App to distinguish local players on
the same device.

## 3. Information processed by the App

The App processes the following limited information locally on the user's device:

- Player nickname: a name chosen by the user to identify a local player profile.
- Preferred language: the language selected for that player or device.
- Game activity: math questions answered, selected game mode, results, score,
  mistakes, time-based performance, leaderboard entries, game history, earned
  coins, unlocked visual styles, button customization choices, and similar
  progress information needed for the game experience.
- App settings: local preferences that make the App remember how it should look
  or behave on the device.

This information is used only to provide the App's educational gameplay,
remember progress, show history and leaderboard information on the device,
support local customization, and make the App more convenient for repeated use.

## 4. Information we do not collect

The App does not collect, request, or transmit the following categories of data
through the App:

- real legal name, unless a user voluntarily types it as a nickname, which is
  not required and not recommended for children;
- email address, telephone number, postal address, or other contact details;
- precise or approximate location;
- photos, videos, microphone recordings, camera data, contacts, calendar data,
  files, messages, or call logs;
- government identifiers, health information, financial information, payment
  card data, or sensitive personal information;
- advertising ID, tracking identifiers, analytics identifiers, or behavioral
  advertising profiles;
- account credentials for any online service.

## 5. No data leaves the device

The App is designed so that the player nickname and game activity remain on the
device. The App does not send that information to Scholtz & Company, jsa, to a
remote server, to an analytics provider, to an advertising network, or to any
other third party.

Because the App does not transmit the information to us, we do not view, sell,
rent, trade, profile, combine, or otherwise commercially exploit the player's
nickname or activity data. We also do not use this information for advertising,
cross-app tracking, user profiling, automated decision-making, or marketing.

Some device-level services are outside the App's control. For example, the app
store, operating system, crash-reporting tools enabled by the platform, device
backup settings, parental controls, or family-library features may process data
under their own terms and privacy policies. This Policy describes the App's own
data handling and not those independent platform services.

## 6. Legal basis and purpose of processing

Where privacy law requires a legal basis, the App processes the limited local
information described above because it is necessary to provide the requested
game functionality and because users expect the App to remember local progress,
scores, player profiles, language settings, and customization choices. The App's
processing is intentionally limited to what is needed for an educational game
and is not used for unrelated purposes.

For children, the App follows a data-minimization approach. It asks only for a
nickname and stores only gameplay information needed to make the educational
experience work. No direct contact with the child is initiated by us through the
App.

## 7. Sharing, sale, and disclosure

We do not share the player nickname or game activity with third parties through
the App. We do not sell personal data. We do not disclose personal data for
targeted advertising. We do not allow third-party advertising or tracking SDKs
to collect data from the App.

If a user independently chooses to show the device screen to another person,
make a screenshot, use operating-system backup, or transfer the device data by
tools outside the App, that action is controlled by the user or device platform,
not by the App.

## 8. Retention and deletion

The player nickname and game activity remain stored locally for as long as the
App remains installed and the relevant local profile or app data is kept on the
device. Users may remove local information by deleting player profiles where the
App provides that option, clearing the App's storage through the operating
system, or uninstalling the App. Uninstalling the App normally removes locally
stored App data from the device, subject to the device operating system and any
backup settings controlled by the user.

Because the App does not send the data to us, we do not maintain a remote copy
that we can search, export, correct, or delete on our servers.

## 9. Security

The App protects children's privacy primarily by avoiding unnecessary collection
and by keeping the limited information local to the device. Local storage is
subject to the security of the device, the operating system, and the user's own
device settings. Parents and guardians should use normal device protections,
such as screen locks, app permissions, family controls, and responsible backup
settings, where appropriate.

No method of electronic storage can be guaranteed to be perfectly secure.
However, the App reduces risk by not transmitting the player's nickname or game
activity to external servers and by not integrating advertising or behavioral
tracking systems.

## 10. Children's privacy and parental control

The App is suitable for children because it is built around arithmetic practice
and local progress. It does not provide open communication between users, does
not publish leaderboard data online, does not expose children to targeted
advertising, and does not request sensitive personal data.

Parents, guardians, and teachers may review the local information visible in the
App by opening the relevant player profile, history, leaderboard, settings, or
customization screens on the device. They may delete or reset local data using
the App's controls where available or by using the device's operating-system
controls.

## 11. International users

The App may be used in different countries and supports multiple languages. The
App's privacy design is the same in every language: player nickname and activity
data are kept locally on the device and are not sent to us through the App.

If a translated version of this Policy differs from the English version, the
English version should be used to resolve the difference to the extent permitted
by applicable law.

## 12. Changes to this Policy

We may update this Policy if the App's features, legal requirements, or app
store requirements change. Any updated version should identify the new effective
date. If a future version of the App ever introduces online services, analytics,
advertising, account features, or data transfer outside the device, this Policy
must be updated before or at the time those features are made available.

## 13. Contact

For privacy questions about the App, contact the developer using the contact
details provided in the Google Play listing or other official distribution page
for Math Master.

## 14. Summary for families

Math Master is intended to be safe for kids. The App stores a nickname and math
game progress on the device so children can play, practise, and see their own
results. The App does not send that information to us, does not sell data, does
not show ads, and does not track children across apps or websites.
